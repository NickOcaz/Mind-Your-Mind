Recognise the signs

Recognizing common mental health issues such as anxiety, depression, and stress is vital for maintaining overall well-being. These issues can manifest in various ways, including persistent sadness, excessive worry, or difficulty sleeping. It's crucial to notice these signs early, as they can significantly impact our daily lives if left unaddressed. Seeking help from mental health professionals can provide essential support and guidance. Alongside professional help, adopting healthy lifestyle practices like regular exercise, mindfulness, and nurturing relationships can make a big difference. By paying attention to our mental health, we take the first step toward a healthier, more balanced life.

Physical symptoms

  • Headaches or dizziness
  • Muscle tension or pain
  • Stomach problems
  • Chest pain or a faster heartbeat
  • Sexual problems

Mental symptoms

  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Struggling to make decisions
  • Feeling overwhelmed
  • Constantly worrying
  • Being forgetful

Changes in behaviour

  • Being irritable and snappy
  • Sleeping too much or too little
  • Eating too much or too little
  • Avoiding certain places or people
  • Drinking or smoking more

Tips for better living

Tips for better living

Recognizing when our mental health might be taking a hit is essential. It's important to remember that even though professional help is invaluable, there are steps we can take ourselves to ease the burden. Small changes like staying active, eating well, taking time to relax, and leaning on friends and family for support can make a big difference. Taking care of our mental health is a journey, and every positive step counts.

Stay Active

  • Exercise Regularly
  • Join a Sports Club
  • Practice Yoga
  • Dance
  • Outdoor Activities

Practice Mindfulness

  • Practice daily mindfulness.
  • Controlled breathing reduces stress.
  • Write down thoughts and feelings.
  • Visualize calming scenarios.
  • Write down three daily gratitudes.

Nurture Relationships

  • Catch-ups with friends and family.
  • Join a Support Group: Share experiences
  • Engage in community service.
  • Plan activities with family.
  • Join online social groups.
